Founding the Bears

Decatur Staleys → Chicago Bears

Halas moved to Decatur to work for the A. E. Staley Company and became player-coach of the company-sponsored football team. In 1920 he represented the club at the meeting that formed the American Professional Football Association, which became the NFL in 1922.

In 1921, Augustus E. Staley agreed to turn over control of the team so Halas could move it to Chicago. The club played as the Chicago Staleys in 1921, then took the name Chicago Bears in 1922—chosen as a nod to the Cubs and the larger size of football players.

Key moments

  • 1920: Organized meeting-era pro football; represented the Staleys at the league’s founding meeting.
  • 1921: Team moved to Chicago; played at Cubs Park (later Wrigley Field).
  • 1922: Renamed the team the Chicago Bears.

Why the move mattered

Chicago offered a bigger fan base, stronger gate receipts, and a clearer identity. The Bears quickly became one of the league’s anchor franchises— and Halas became one of pro football’s most visible builders.

“If baseball players are Cubs, then football players must be Bears!” — a later recollection attributed to Halas about the 1922 name change.
