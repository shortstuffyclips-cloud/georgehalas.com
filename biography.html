Biography

George Halas in one view

Halas (1895–1983) was an American football player, coach, and executive best known for founding and leading the Chicago Bears. He represented the team at the 1920 meeting that formed the league that became the NFL, moved the franchise to Chicago in 1921, and renamed it the Bears in 1922.

Why he mattered

  • Franchise founder: guided the team from small-company “works team” roots into a Chicago institution.
  • Long-term coach: one of the earliest examples of a head coach shaping both strategy and culture.
  • Business operator: handled operations in an era when many teams struggled financially.
  • League advocate: promoted the sport and helped stabilize pro football.
