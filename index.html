Founder. Coach. Architect.

George “Papa Bear” Halas

George Halas was not simply present at the birth of professional football — he helped design it. As a founder of the National Football League, the driving force behind the Chicago Bears, and one of the sport’s most enduring leaders, Halas shaped American football for more than sixty years.

This site explores the full scope of his influence — from early league survival and championship dominance to rule innovations, business decisions, and the legacy that still defines the NFL today.

A foundational figure in American sports

Few individuals have influenced a sport as deeply as George Halas. His vision helped professional football survive its early years, thrive in the television age, and become the most powerful sports league in North America.

This archive presents Halas not as a myth, but as a builder — one decision at a time.
