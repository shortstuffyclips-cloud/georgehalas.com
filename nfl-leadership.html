NFL Leadership

Building a league, not just a team

In 1920, Halas attended and represented the Staleys at the meeting in Canton that formed the American Professional Football Association—later renamed the NFL. Beyond the Bears, his legacy includes helping stabilize and legitimize the league during decades when pro football fought for attention and survival.

How early pro football leadership worked

Early league governance was often informal compared with modern sports. Owners and representatives negotiated rules, schedules, and standards face-to-face—trying to keep the league credible and solvent.

  • Represented the Staleys/Bears at foundational league moments.
  • Advocated for professionalism in operations and public image.
  • Long-term influence through continuity—decades with one franchise during the league’s growth.
The Pro Football Hall of Fame notes Halas was associated with the Bears and the NFL from their inception in 1920 until his death in 1983.
